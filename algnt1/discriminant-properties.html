Chapter 57 More properties of the discriminant

I’ll remind you that the discriminant of a number field K is given by

ΔK≔det[σ1⁢(α1)…σn⁢(α1)⋮⋱⋮σ1⁢(αn)…σn⁢(αn)]2

where α1, …, αn is a ℤ-basis for K, and the σi are the n embeddings of K into ℂ.

Several examples, properties, and equivalent definitions follow.

57.1 A few harder problems to think about

Problem 57A (Discriminant of cyclotomic field).

Let p be an odd rational prime and ζp a primitive pth root of unity. Let K=ℚ⁢(ζp). Show that

ΔK=(−1)p−12⁢pp−2.

Direct linear algebra computation.

Problem 57B (Trace representation of ΔK).
††margin:

Let α1, …, αn be a basis for 𝒪K. Prove that

ΔK=det[TrK/ℚ⁡(α12)TrK/ℚ⁡(α1⁢α2)…TrK/ℚ⁡(α1⁢αn)TrK/ℚ⁡(α2⁢α1)TrK/ℚ⁡(α22)…TrK/ℚ⁡(α2⁢αn)⋮⋮⋱⋮TrK/ℚ⁡(αn⁢α1)TrK/ℚ⁡(αn⁢α2)…TrK/ℚ⁡(αn⁢αn)].

In particular, ΔK is an integer. Let M be the “embedding” matrix. Look at M⊤⁢M, where M⊤ is the transpose matrix.

Problem 57C (Root representation of ΔK).

The discriminant of a quadratic polynomial A⁢x2+B⁢x+C is defined as B2−4⁢A⁢C. More generally, the polynomial discriminant of a polynomial f∈ℤ⁢[x] of degree n is

Δ⁢(f)≔c2⁢n−2⁢∏1≤i<j≤n(zi−zj)2

where z1,…,zn are the roots of f, and c is the leading coefficient of f.

Suppose K is monogenic with 𝒪K=ℤ⁢[θ]. Let f denote the minimal polynomial of θ (hence monic). Show that

ΔK=Δ⁢(f).

Vandermonde matrices.

Problem 57D.

Show that if K≠ℚ is a number field then |ΔK|>1. MK≥1 must hold. Bash.

Problem 57E (Brill’s theorem).

For a number field K with signature (r1,r2), show that ΔK>0 if and only if r2 is even.

Problem 57F (Stickelberger theorem).
††margin:

Let K be a number field. Prove that

ΔK≡0⁢ or ⁢1(mod4).
