78.1 Synopsis

Group theory was a strange creature in the early 19th century. During the 19th century, a group was literally defined as a subset of GL‚Å°(n) or of Sn. Indeed, the word ‚Äúgroup‚Äù hadn‚Äôt been invented yet. This may sound ludicrous, but it was true ‚Äì Sylow developed his theorems without this notion. Only much later was the abstract definition of a group given, an abstract set G which was independent of any embedding into Sn, and an object in its own right.

We are about to make the same type of change for our affine varieties. Rather than thinking of them as an object locked into an ambient space ùî∏n we are instead going to try to make them into an object in their own right. Specifically, for us an affine variety will become a topological space equipped with a ring of functions for each of its open sets: this is why we call it a ringed space.

The bit about the topological space is not too drastic. The key insight is the addition of the ring of functions. For example, consider the double point from last chapter.

78.7 Baby ringed spaces

In summary, given an affine variety V we have:

  • ‚Ä¢

  • ‚Ä¢

Definition 78.7.1.

Therefore, affine varieties are baby ringed spaces.

The reason this is called a ‚Äúbaby ringed space‚Äù is that in a ringed space, the rings ùí™V‚Å¢(U) can actually be any rings, but they have to satisfy a set of fairly technical conditions. When this happens, it‚Äôs the ùí™V that does all the work; we think of ùí™V as a type of functor called a sheaf.

Since we are only studying affine/projective/quasi-projective varieties for the next chapters, we will just refer to these as baby ringed spaces so that we don‚Äôt have to deal with the entire definition. The key concept is that we want to think of these varieties as intrinsic objects, free of any embedding. A baby ringed space is philosophically the correct thing to do.

Anyways, affine varieties are baby ringed spaces (V,ùí™V). In the next chapter we‚Äôll meet projective and quasi-projective varieties, which give more such examples of (baby) ringed spaces.
