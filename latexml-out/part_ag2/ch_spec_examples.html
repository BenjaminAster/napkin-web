86.14.iv Stalk above the origin (tricky)

The stalk above the origin (x,y) is interesting, and has some ideas in it we won‚Äôt be able to explore fully without talking about localizations of modules. The localization is given by

If you like category theory, it is thus a fibered product

We really ought to be able to do the same as the stalk: we wish to say that

ùí™Spec‚Å°k‚Å¢[x,y]/(x‚Å¢y),(x,y)‚âÖk‚Å¢[x](x)√ókk‚Å¢[y](y).

English translation: a ‚Äútypical‚Äù germ ought to look like 3+xx2+7+4+y3y2+y+7, with the x and y parts decoupled. Equivalently, the stalk should consist of pairs of x-germs and y-germs that agree at the origin.

In fact, this is true! This might come as a surprise, but let‚Äôs see why we expect this. Suppose we take the germ

If we hold our breath, we could imagine expanding it as a geometric series: 1+(x+y)+(x+y)2+‚Ä¶. As x‚Å¢y=0, this just becomes 1+x+x2+x3+‚ãØ+y+y2+y3+‚Ä¶. This is nonsense (as written), but nonetheless it suggests the conjecture
