69.3 Limits

Weâ€™ve defined cones over discrete sets of Xi and over forks. It turns out you can also define a cone over any general diagram of objects and arrows; we specify a projection from A to each object and require that the projections from A commute with the arrows in the diagram. (For example, a cone over a fork is a diagram with two edges and two arrows.) If you then demand the cone be universal, you have the extremely general definition of a limit. As always, these are unique up to unique isomorphism. We can also define the dual notion of a colimit in the same way.
